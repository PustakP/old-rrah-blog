    It is ironic that the human body can find such great joy in suffering. Even more surprising is, we are the ones who have given meaning to the word “suffering”.
    As one foot falls after the other. Your calves tense up and the shoes break off the dreary asphalt once more. A breath enters your scorched lungs, and that stranded drop of sweat on your chin falls back to the ground, probably where it came from.
    Your feet feel like they have been pressed upon hot coal, a burning sensation courses through your throat. ‘I might vomit’, you think, but you don’t. The almost hilarious timing of this tenaciousness leaves you no room to think. Almost mechanically, you rise you foot once again, and take a breath.

    ‘Deep breaths, every two steps.’, you tell yourself, checking your shoulders, fixing whats wrong in your running form. It matters naught, for you know that you shall fall soon. However, the slight euphoria one gets as the control of taking a breath is transferred from the body to the mind. As the person finally feels whole, ridding of all thoughts and being one with the earth, all for the simple task of getting from a point to another.
    But is it so simple, if we forget what the point is? Whatever is the point of it all, it does not matter, because all which does, is to breath, and to take another step.
    To run is to breathe
    And to breathe is to run.
